MAGMA

Multiplier-Augmented Geometric Motion Algorithm

César E. Ramos Chuquiure, Vansh Thakur, Challen Enninful Adu, and Ram Vasudevan

ROAHM Lab, University of Michigan

Abstract

MAGMA (Multiplier-Augmented Geometric Motion Algorithm) is a general trajectory optimizer based on the Affine Geometric Heat Flow (AGHF). It incorporates an augmented Lagrangian framework that is solved via a primal-dual flow.

Solving optimal control problems for high-dimensional systems with constraints is challenging due to nonlinear dynamics and non-convex feasible sets. Prior AGHF-based approaches relied on penalty or barrier formulations, which required extensive hyperparameter tuning and struggled when cost minimization and constraint satisfaction competed.

MAGMA features

Dramatically Reduced Manual Tuning

The dual flow automatically accomodates constraint violations, resulting in a fundamentally different optimization landscape.

Robust Initialization

Barrier methods can be give rise to numerically large values in the gradient for bad initial guesses, MAGMA circumvents that completely!.

High Accuracy

MAGMA retains and betters the solution quality of previous AGHF formulations, while simultaneously being better than classical DDP methods

General Constraints

Handles general nonlinear constraints and arbitrary cost functions, going beyond previous AGHF formulations.

Method

MAGMA extends the AGHF equation to handle constraints by defining an augmented primal-dual AGHF. It establishes a theoretical characterization of the primal-dual AGHF, proving that MAGMA is a gradient flow on an appropriately defined energy.

The numerical implementation utilizes a pseudospectral method-of-lines approach. To handle constraints dynamically, it approximates constraint derivatives using a smooth activation function.

Two-Phase Solver

Phase 1 Find a feasible trajectory
(no control effort minimization)
→
Phase 2 Refine with control effort
(u²) minimization
→
Result Optimized, constraint-satisfying trajectory

Results

MAGMA was validated across a variety of challenging trajectory generation problems featuring obstacles, state limits, and actuation limits, and evaluated against state-of-the-art solvers.

Task-Based Scenarios

MAGMA was tested in practical, task-oriented scenarios involving cuboid obstacles, such as reaching into shelves and placing items in confined bins, reflecting real-world industrial and domestic deployments.
